Frequently Asked Questions

Common Questions

The American Academy of Pediatric Dentistry recommends bringing your child for their first dental visit by their first birthday or when their first tooth appears. Early visits help establish good oral health habits and make your child comfortable with dental care.

You can help prepare your child by:
  • Reading books about dental visits
  • Playing "dentist" at home
  • Maintaining a positive attitude about dental care
  • Scheduling appointments during their best time of day

During a routine check-up, we:
  • Examine teeth for cavities and growth
  • Clean and polish teeth
  • Provide fluoride treatment if needed
  • Offer tips for proper brushing and flossing
  • Make the visit fun and educational!

We recommend check-ups every 6 months for most children. However, some children might need more frequent visits based on their oral health needs and development. Dr. Sansrati will create a personalized care schedule for your child.
